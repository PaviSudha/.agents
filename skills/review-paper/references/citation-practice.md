# Citation Practice — Journal-Standard Academic References

Derived from published exemplars:
- Salo et al. (2024) *European Journal of Operational Research* — Author-Year
- Zhao et al. (2025) *Expert Systems with Applications* — Author-Year with DOIs

---

## STEP 1: IDENTIFY YOUR JOURNAL'S CITATION SYSTEM

Two systems exist. **Never mix them.**

| System | Also called | In-text look | Reference list |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Author-Year** | Harvard, APA | (Markowitz, 1952) | Alphabetical by surname |
| **Numbered** | Vancouver, IEEE | [1] or (1) | Ordered by first appearance |

**For OR/management/finance/AI journals (EJOR, ESWA, Management Science, Operations Research, Journal of Banking & Finance):** use **Author-Year**.

**Confirm before writing:** Check the journal's Guide for Authors or look at 2–3 published papers in that journal.

---

## IN-TEXT CITATIONS — AUTHOR-YEAR SYSTEM

### The Two Citation Modes

**Mode 1 — Author as grammatical subject** (name is part of the sentence):
> Markowitz (1952) observed that various utility functions can be approximated by a quadratic function.
> Zhao et al. (2025) systematically reviewed 227 articles.

**Mode 2 — Parenthetical** (name is supporting evidence at end of clause):
> The mean–variance model remains foundational in portfolio theory (Markowitz, 1952).
> Several reviews have addressed AI in forecasting (Kehinde et al., 2023; Kumbure et al., 2022).

---

### Author Count Rules

| Authors | In-text (Mode 1) | Parenthetical (Mode 2) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 author | Markowitz (1952) | (Markowitz, 1952) |
| 2 authors | Salo and Keisler (2011) | (Salo & Keisler, 2011) |
| 3+ authors | Zhao et al. (2025) | (Zhao et al., 2025) |

**Critical rule:** Use **"and"** (spelled out) when the authors are part of the sentence. Use **"&"** (ampersand) only inside parentheses.

- ✅ "Salo and Keisler (2011) introduced..."
- ✅ "(Salo & Keisler, 2011)"
- ❌ "Salo & Keisler (2011) introduced..." ← ampersand in running text is wrong

---

### Multiple Citations in One Parenthesis

When citing more than one paper to support the same claim:
- Separate with **semicolons**
- Order: **alphabetically** by first author surname (most common in OR journals) or chronologically (check journal style)

> (Artzner et al., 1999; Lim et al., 2011; Szegö, 2002)
> (Markowitz, 1952; Salo et al., 2024; Zhao et al., 2025)

**Not:** (Markowitz, 1952, Salo et al., 2024) ← commas between citations is wrong; use semicolons

---

### Same Author, Same Year

If citing two papers by the same author(s) published the same year, add letter suffixes:
> Liesiö et al. (2020a) and Liesiö et al. (2020b) address different portfolio problems.

Apply the same suffix in the reference list. Alphabetize the titles to assign a/b/c.

---

### Same Author, Different Years

List chronologically within the parenthesis:
> (Markowitz, 1952, 1999, 2014)

---

### Secondary Citations (citing a source you haven't read)

Avoid. Always trace back to the original source. If unavoidable:
> "As cited in Zhao et al. (2025), Markowitz (1952) established..."

Most journals discourage secondary citations; do not use them in the reference list.

---

### Citation Placement

**Before the period — for evidence citations:**
> Portfolio optimization has grown substantially (Salo et al., 2024).

**After the author name — for author-as-subject:**
> Markowitz (1952) introduced the mean–variance framework.

**Mid-sentence — when only part of the sentence comes from the source:**
> While Samuelson (1970) showed that the mean–variance model is most useful when risk is low, Kane (1982) examined an enhanced model for discrete-time rebalancing.

## REFERENCE LIST — FULL FORMAT GUIDE

### General Rules (apply to all reference types)

- List is **alphabetical** by first author's surname
- **All authors** are listed — never use "et al." in the reference list
- **Year** in parentheses immediately after the authors
- **Sentence case** for article and book titles (only proper nouns capitalized)
- **Title Case** for journal names (every major word capitalized)
- **DOI** always included when available, formatted as a hyperlink: `https://doi.org/XXXXX`
- **No abbreviation** of journal names (write in full) unless journal explicitly requires it

---

### Format 1: Journal Article (most common)

**Formula:**
```
Surname, I. N., Surname, I. N., & Surname, I. N. (Year). Title of article in sentence case.
Journal Name in Title Case, Volume(Issue), first page–last page.
https://doi.org/XXXXX
```

**Example from Zhao et al. (2025):**
> Akyildirim, E., Bariviera, A. F., Nguyen, D. K., & Sensoy, A. (2022). Forecasting high-frequency stock returns: A comparison of alternative methods. *Annals of Operations Research*, *313*(2), 639–690. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10479-021-04464-8

**Breakdown:**
- Authors: Last, First Initial. Middle Initial. — all authors
- Year: (2022). — with period after closing parenthesis
- Title: sentence case — only first word and proper nouns capitalized
- Journal: *Annals of Operations Research* — italicized, Title Case
- Volume(Issue): *313*(2) — volume italicized, issue in parentheses not italicized
- Pages: 639–690 — en-dash, not hyphen
- DOI: full URL format

**When there is no issue number:** `*313*, 639–690.`
**When article has an article number:** `*186*, Article 115724.`

---

### Format 2: Book

```
Surname, I. N. (Year). Book title in sentence case. Publisher.
```

**Example:**
> Markowitz, H. M. (1959). *Portfolio selection: Efficient diversification of investments*. Wiley.

> Mansini, R., Ogryczak, W., & Speranza, M. G. (2015). *Linear and mixed integer programming for portfolio optimization*. Springer.

---

### Format 3: Book Chapter (edited volume)

```
Surname, I. N. (Year). Chapter title in sentence case. In I. N. Editor & I. N. Editor (Eds.),
Book title in sentence case (pp. first–last). Publisher.
```

**Example:**
> Kloeber, J. (2011). Current and cutting edge methods of portfolio decision analysis in pharmaceutical R&D. In A. Salo, J. Keisler, & A. Morton (Eds.), *Portfolio decision analysis: Improved methods for resource allocation* (pp. 283–331). Springer.

---

### Format 4: Conference Paper

```
Surname, I. N. (Year). Title of paper in sentence case. In I. N. Editor (Ed.),
Proceedings of Conference Name (pp. first–last). Publisher.
```

Or if no editor:
```
Surname, I. N. (Year, Month). Title of paper. Paper presented at Conference Name, City, Country.
```

---

### Format 5: Working Paper / Preprint

```
Surname, I. N. (Year). Title of paper (Working Paper No. XXX). Institution.
https://doi.org/XXXXX
```

Or for SSRN:
```
Surname, I. N. (Year). Title of paper. Available at SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=XXXXXXX
```

**Example from Salo et al.:**
> Guo, D., Boyle, P. P., Weng, C., & Wirjanto, T. S. (2018). Eigen portfolio selection: A robust approach to Sharpe ratio maximization. Available at SSRN 3070416.

---

### Format 6: In-Press / Forthcoming Article

```
Surname, I. N. (in press). Title of article. Journal Name in Title Case.
https://doi.org/XXXXX
```

Or if accepted but no DOI yet:
```
Surname, I. N. (in press). Title of article. Journal Name in Title Case.
```

**Example from Salo et al.:**
> Abid, I., Urom, C., Peillex, J., Karmani, M., & Ndubuisi, G. (2023). PGP for portfolio optimization: Application to ESG index family. *Annals of Operations Research*, in press.

## COMMON CITATION ERRORS — DIAGNOSIS AND FIX

| Error | Example of error | Correct version |
|---|---|---|
| Ampersand in running text | "Salo & Keisler (2011) showed…" | "Salo and Keisler (2011) showed…" |
| "And" inside parentheses | "(Salo and Keisler, 2011)" | "(Salo & Keisler, 2011)" |
| Comma instead of semicolon between citations | "(Markowitz, 1952, Sharpe, 1964)" | "(Markowitz, 1952; Sharpe, 1964)" |
| "et al." with only 2 authors | "Salo et al. (2011)" for a 2-author paper | "Salo and Keisler (2011)" |
| "Et al." (capitalized) | "Et al." | "et al." — always lowercase |
| Wrong case in article title | "Portfolio Selection" | "Portfolio selection" (sentence case) |
| Abbreviated journal name | "EJOR" | "European Journal of Operational Research" |
| Missing DOI | Reference ends at page numbers | Add https://doi.org/… |
| Hyphen instead of en-dash in page range | "639-690" | "639–690" |
| Missing issue number | "313, 639–690" | "313(2), 639–690" |
| Citing textbook for an original result | Citing Jorion (2006) for VaR definition | Cite the paper that introduced VaR |
| "et al." in reference list | "Markowitz, H., et al. (1993)…" | List all authors: "Markowitz, H., Todd, P., Xu, G., & Yamane, Y. (1993)…" |
| Self-citation overuse | 8+ self-citations in a 30-reference paper | Keep self-citation ≤ 15–20% of references |
| Secondary citation in reference list | Citing "Hegel, as cited in Zhao et al." | Go to the original source |

## WHAT TO CITE AND WHEN

### Cite the original paper, not the textbook

If claiming "VaR was introduced as a risk measure," cite the paper that introduced it — not a textbook that explains it.

**Wrong:** "VaR is a risk measure (Jorion, 2006)" when Jorion is a textbook explaining VaR
**Right:** Cite the paper that originally proposed VaR for portfolio optimization

### Cite methods where they are introduced

When describing a method used in reviewed papers:
- Cite the paper that *introduced* the method (original contribution)
- Then cite papers in your review that *applied* it (application examples)

> "The PRISMA framework (Shamseer et al., 2015) was adopted for literature screening. Several portfolio reviews have followed this approach (Zhao et al., 2025; Mandal & Thakur, 2024)."

### Cite claims, not sections

Every factual or evaluative claim needs a citation. Questions to ask:
- "Could another researcher verify this claim by reading the cited paper?" → If no, find a better citation
- "Is this a well-known mathematical fact or definition?" → No citation needed
- "Is this an interpretive claim about what the literature shows?" → Cite multiple papers supporting it

### What does NOT need a citation

- Mathematical definitions and standard formulas (e.g., the definition of variance)
- Historical facts more than 50 years old and universally known
- Your own interpretive synthesis (what the review's argument is)
- Claims immediately supported by data you present in the same paper

---

## CITING IN THE INTRODUCTION'S COMPARISON TABLE

When the Introduction contains a literature comparison table (Table 1 type), every prior review listed in the table must also be cited in the surrounding text. The table citation and the in-text citation must be consistent — same year, same author formatting.

> "Table 1 summarizes six review articles… Mandal & Thakur (2024) focus on higher-order moments in portfolio selection. Migliavacca et al. (2023) emphasize portfolio diversification."

---

## REFERENCE LIST ORDERING — EDGE CASES

| Situation | Rule |
|---|---|
| Same surname, different first names | Alphabetize by first initial: Brown, A. before Brown, C. |
| Same first author, co-authors differ | Alphabetize by second author surname |
| Same author(s), different years | Earlier year first: (2019) before (2022) |
| Same author(s), same year | Add a/b/c: (2020a), (2020b) — alphabetize by title to assign |
| Single author vs. two authors (same first author) | Single author first, regardless of year |

---

## QUICK REFERENCE: IN-TEXT CITATION CHEAT SHEET

```
1 author:
   Subject:      Markowitz (1952) showed...
   Parenthetical: ...(Markowitz, 1952).

2 authors:
   Subject:      Salo and Keisler (2011) argued...
   Parenthetical: ...(Salo & Keisler, 2011).

3+ authors:
   Subject:      Zhao et al. (2025) reviewed...
   Parenthetical: ...(Zhao et al., 2025).

Multiple citations:
   ...(Markowitz, 1952; Salo et al., 2024; Zhao et al., 2025).
   [alphabetical, separated by semicolons]

Same author, same year:
   (Liesiö et al., 2020a) and (Liesiö et al., 2020b)

Same author, multiple years:
   (Markowitz, 1952, 1999, 2014)
```
